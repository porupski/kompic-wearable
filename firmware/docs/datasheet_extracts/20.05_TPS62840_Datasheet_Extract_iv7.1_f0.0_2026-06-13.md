## Registers (firmware-critical)

**Not applicable** — the TPS62840 has no programmable register file. All configuration is set in hardware:
- Output voltage: **R_SET on VSET pin** (read once at start-up).
- Mode: **MODE pin level** (read continuously; changeable at runtime).
- Stop: **STOP pin level** (read continuously; changeable at runtime).
- Enable: **EN pin level**.

The R2D (resistor-to-digital) converter reads R_SET once during the t_STARTUP_DELAY window, latches the V_OUT setting internally, then **disconnects R_SET to prevent leakage** [p. 12 §8.3.4]. This is why C_VSET parasitic must stay ≤ 100 pF — extra capacitance corrupts the R2D measurement.

**Implication:** FW cannot change V_OUT at runtime. To change V_OUT, the EN pin must be cycled (full power-down → power-up) **with a different R_SET physically connected**. Not a runtime feature.

## Initialization sequence

This is a power-management chip — "initialization" is hardware-state-machine driven, not FW-driven. The flow below shows what happens when SYS first rises and EN goes HIGH (per [p. 12, §8.3; p. 13 §8.3.5]):

```
1. (Hardware) Battery connects → SYS rail rises through UVLO threshold (1.72–1.8 V rising).
2. (Hardware) EN pin sees HIGH (tied to SYS in v7.2). Internal logic powers up.
3. t_STARTUP_DELAY:
     - 200 µs typ if V_IN was already stable before EN went HIGH.
     - up to 10 ms if V_IN ramps simultaneously with EN [p. 8].
   During this window, the R2D converter reads R_SET, latches the V_OUT code.
4. Soft-start (t_SS): switching begins. Current is bounded to I_LIMF_SS = 225 mA typ
   (300 mA worst case) for the entire soft-start phase. V_OUT ramps to target.
     - t_SS = 120 µs typ (no load).
     - Soft-start can extend up to t_SS_ILIMF ≈ 700 µs max if heavy load present.
5. V_OUT in regulation. Switch current limit relaxes to I_LIMF = 1.2 A typ.
6. R2D converter disconnects R_SET → 0 leakage on VSET.
7. EN-pin internal pulldown disconnects → EN draws ~0 from VIN.
8. Steady-state operation:
     - MODE = LOW → DCS-Control auto-PFM/PWM. I_Q = 60–150 nA depending on V_OUT/V_IN.
     - MODE = HIGH → forced PWM. I_Q ≈ 3 mA.
```

**Firmware action items at boot** (none required for operation — but for awareness):
- The 3.2 V rail is up before the ESP-S3 sees its own power-on-reset. No FW "rail-up" handshake needed.
- If FW wants to verify the rail came up correctly, route VOS or VOUT to an ESP ADC pin for sanity check (currently not in v7.2 — Open Q3).
- If FW needs to **cycle** the rail (e.g., to recover from a sensor lock-up), it must own GPIO control of EN — *currently EN is hardwired to SYS in v7.2*. See Open Q4.

**Critical timing constraint:** during the t_STARTUP_DELAY window the VSET trace must be quiet (no large transients, no high-Z parasitic > 100 pF). Once R_SET is latched, the line goes high-Z and any noise on the trace afterwards is harmless.

### Mode selection [p. 12, §8.3.3; p. 15, §8.4]

| MODE pin | Behavior | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| LOW (GND) | **Power-Save Mode** — auto PFM at light load, PWM at heavy load. Seamless transition. Switching freq scales with load (sub-Hz at I_OUT < 1 µA, up to 1.8 MHz). | **Default for wearable.** Best efficiency at light loads (1 µA – 10 mA). |
| HIGH (V_IN) | **Forced PWM** — 1.8 MHz constant regardless of load. Lower ripple, RF-friendly, but I_Q ≈ 3 mA. | **Brief windows only** — during ADC reads, RF transmit, or critical analog measurements. |

**MODE can be toggled at runtime** [p. 12, §8.3.3]. In FW with GPIO-driven MODE: hold LOW for normal operation; drive HIGH 100–500 µs before a noise-sensitive sample, then return to LOW.

### Stop mode [p. 14, §8.3.9]

| STOP pin | Behavior |
|---|---|
| LOW (GND) | Normal switching. |
| HIGH (V_IN) | **Switching halts immediately after the current cycle.** Output rides on C_OUT charge. I_Q jumps to ≈ 70 µA (typ, max 175 µA). Output voltage will droop at rate set by C_OUT and load: ΔV/Δt = I_LOAD / C_OUT (e.g., 10 µF cap, 10 mA load → 1 V/ms — very fast). |

**Use case:** noise-free instant for an ADC sample. Hold STOP HIGH for ~50 µs, take ADC sample, release STOP — the rail droops a few mV, then snaps back when switching resumes (no soft-start). FW must guarantee STOP-HIGH duration is short enough that V_OUT droop doesn't cross the load's BOR threshold.

### 100 % mode [p. 15, §8.4.3]

When V_IN approaches V_OUT, the duty cycle ramps to 100 % and the high-side MOSFET stays on continuously. V_OUT = V_IN − I_OUT × (R_DSon + R_L). With R_DSon_max = 600 mΩ and DCR 116 mΩ (per v7.2 inductor DFE201612E-2R2M):

| I_OUT | Voltage drop | V_IN needed to maintain 3.2 V output |
|---|---|---|
| 10 mA | 7 mV | 3.207 V |
| 100 mA | 72 mV | 3.272 V |
| 200 mA | 143 mV | 3.343 V |
| 500 mA | 358 mV | 3.558 V |
| 750 mA | 537 mV | 3.737 V |

**Implication:** as the Li-ion battery drains toward 3.4 V, the 3.2 V rail is barely regulated at moderate loads — it follows V_IN minus a drop. The buck stops "regulating" in the usual sense (it's just a pass-through). I_Q in 100 % mode = **120 nA typ** [p. 7]. The V_OUT setpoint is also increased by **+30 mV** as it approaches the rail [p. 15] — hysteresis to make the transition clean. See WARN-04.

### Output discharge [p. 14, §8.3.7]

When EN = LOW (or UVLO trips), an internal MOSFET sinks current from VOS to GND. Datasheet spec: **I_DISCHARGE_VOS = 35 mA typ, 44 mA max** at V_OUT = 1.8 V [p. 8]. **Discharge does NOT activate during thermal shutdown** — only on EN-LOW or UVLO. Active as long as V_IN > 0.7 V.

### Switch current limit & short-circuit protection [p. 14, §8.3.6]

- High-side static limit **I_LIMF = 1.2 A typ** (1.0 A min, 1.4 A max).
- Low-side limit **1.0 A typ**.
- Negative current limit (forced PWM) **I_LIMN = 533 mA**.
- Cycle-by-cycle protection. When I_LIMF trips: HS off → LS on → inductor current decreases → when I < low-side limit, HS on again.
- During soft-start, limit is reduced to **I_LIMF_SS = 225 mA typ**.
- Internal propagation delay t_I_LIM_DELAY = **50 ns** typ → actual peak can exceed static limit by ΔI = V_L × t / L = (V_IN − V_OUT) × 50 ns / 2.2 µH ≈ 11 mA for V_IN−V_OUT = 0.5 V. Negligible margin.

### Thermal shutdown [p. 14, §8.3.8]

- T_SD = **160 °C typ** rising (5 °C hysteresis → resumes at ≈ 155 °C).
- Both HS and LS FETs off when active.
- On recovery, soft-start cycle without re-reading R_SET. V_OUT setpoint is preserved.
- **Power-Save Mode disables thermal shutdown** (the converter is essentially off at no-load anyway). FW should not rely on thermal shutdown if MODE = LOW under stuck-load fault conditions — design for the load to fail safe.

## Interrupts

**Not applicable.** The TPS62840 has no interrupt output. There is no PWRGOOD pin on this part. If FW needs to know "rail is up", it must:
1. Wait t_STARTUP_DELAY + t_SS (≤ 1 ms worst-case from EN → regulation), **or**
2. Sense V_OUT via an ADC pin (not currently routed in v7.2 — see Open Q3).

The MCU itself (ESP-S3) has its own internal BOR detector on its V_DD pin, which would catch a buck failure as a brown-out. That is the de-facto "rail health monitor" in the current v7.2 design.

**System budget for the 3.2 V rail:**
- Deep sleep (MODE = LOW, all sensors off): rail load is essentially ESP-S3 light-sleep + RTC = a few µA. Buck I_Q dominates at ≤ 150 nA. **Total rail-side draw ≈ 5–10 µA** depending on what's still alive on the 3.2 V rail.
- Active runtime (MCU running, display off): rail load 5–30 mA. Buck efficiency at 10 mA ≈ 88–92 % (Fig 16 / 19 at V_OUT = 1.8 V / 3.3 V respectively). I_Q is irrelevant at this load.
- Active screen-on with backlight: rail load possibly 50–150 mA (depends on display pixel duty). Efficiency ~88–92 %.
- **High-current burst** (full sensor scan + radio + display): up to 200–500 mA. Forced PWM in this regime is fine; PFM also fine (auto-transition).
- **Forced PWM trap:** if FW accidentally leaves MODE = HIGH during deep-sleep, the buck draws 3 mA continuously → battery dies in ≈ 1 month from this alone (300 mAh battery ÷ 3 mA = 100 h ≈ 4 days, actually). **WARN-05.**

**Layout-critical rules** [p. 28, §11.1]:
1. C_IN ground return must be very short to GND pin — most critical placement.
2. VOS line is a sensitive high-impedance trace — route **away** from SW node and other noisy traces. Connect to C_OUT positive pad directly.
3. SW node should be small and well-shielded; it's the only noisy trace.
4. Use wide, short traces for V_IN and V_OUT paths to minimize ESR.

## Datasheet notes

- **DCS-Control topology** [p. 11, §8.1] = "Direct Control with Seamless Transition into Power-Save Mode". An AC feedback loop senses V_OUT directly (via VOS pin) into a fast comparator → very fast load transient response and seamless PFM ↔ PWM transitions. The DC accuracy loop runs in parallel for steady-state regulation.
- **V_OUT accuracy (PWM mode, I_OUT = 0):** ±1.5 % for V_OUT ≥ 1.8 V, ±2.0 % for V_OUT ≤ 1.55 V [p. 8]. At 3.2 V target → ±48 mV worst-case.
- **DC load regulation:** 0 %/mA in PWM mode (perfect regulation in steady-state) [p. 8].
- **DC line regulation:** 0 %/V across V_IN range in PWM mode [p. 8].
- **Output discharge active during UVLO** [p. 14] — useful detail: a brown-out actively pulls V_OUT low, so loads on the 3.2 V rail see a clean fall, not a slow droop.
- **Switching frequency variation** [p. 22, Figs 35–40] — at extreme light load in PFM, the device can switch as little as 10 µHz (one cycle per ~28 hours). This is not a fault; it's the intended deep-sleep idle behavior.
- **V_OUT bumped +30 mV near 100 % duty** [p. 15] — hysteresis to avoid mode chatter as battery droops.
- **R2D conversion happens at every wakeup from UVLO** [p. 13, §8.3.5] — if the rail browns out then recovers, R_SET is re-read. This means a noisy VSET trace during a brown-out recovery could cause V_OUT to land on a different code. WARN-06.
- **EVM PCB layout exists for all 3 packages** [p. 28–29, §11.2, Figs 61–63]. Use Fig 61 for v7.2 (DLC).
- **Production status:** Active, RoHS, T&R packaging (3000/reel for DLC). Lead finish: SN/Call TI. MSL Level-1, peak reflow 260 °C [Package Option Addendum, p. 1].
- **Compatible parts in same family**: TPS62841 (0.8–1.55 V output), TPS62842 (1.8/2.0/2.2/2.4–3.6 V output, DGR only), TPS62849 (fixed 3.4 V, DLC only). Not pin-compatible across all variants — the YBG package omits MODE/STOP. **Confirm BOM line for TPS62840DLC specifically.** [p. 3]
- **Trademark:** DCS-Control is a TI trademark [p. 30, §12.3]. Not a generic term.
- **Datasheet revision:** Rev. D, March 2020. Latest. Rev. C added DGR package and low-side current limit spec; Rev. D added efficiency graphs and updated comparison table.

## Warnings & Errata

| ID | Title | Source | Severity | Description / Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| WARN-01 | **v7.2 calls R_SET a "100 kΩ divider" — it's a single resistor, and the optimal E96 value is 102 kΩ** | [p. 13, Table 1] | **MED** | The output voltage is set by **one resistor from VSET to GND** (not a divider — there's no upper resistor). The R_SET → V_OUT relationship is discrete-coded by an internal R2D converter, with a fixed E96 window per output voltage code. For 3.2 V the window is 97.92–106.08 kΩ; **nominal is 102 kΩ, not 100 kΩ**. A literal 100 kΩ part falls inside the 3.2 V band, but only just (≈ 2 % margin to the lower edge). **Mitigation:** (a) update v7.2 BOM/wording to "R_SET = 102 kΩ, 1 %, ±200 ppm/°C, E96" — re-source if currently 100 kΩ 5 %. (b) Confirm the existing PCB footprint allows the BOM swap. (c) During bench bring-up, measure V_OUT — must read 3.152–3.248 V (±1.5 %) at no-load PWM. |
| WARN-02 | **STOP pin is for noise-free measurements, NOT a low-power mode** | [p. 14, §8.3.9; p. 7 I_Q_VIN_STOP] | **MED** | A naïve reading of "STOP mode" might suggest battery-saving. **It is not.** I_Q in STOP = **70 µA typ**, vs **60 nA typ** in normal PFM Power-Save Mode — STOP is 1 000× **worse** for battery life. STOP exists only to silence switching noise during noise-sensitive sample windows (ADC, RF). **Mitigation:** (a) document this explicitly in firmware comments and v7.2 §POWER. (b) Confirm v7.2 ties STOP to GND (normal operation), or routes to an ESP-S3 GPIO with default-LOW driver. **Never default STOP HIGH**. (c) If FW uses STOP for noise gating, keep STOP-HIGH duration < 100 µs and budget V_OUT droop = I_LOAD / C_OUT × t_STOP (e.g., 50 mA × 50 µs / 10 µF = 250 mV droop — too much; load must be quieted first). |
| WARN-03 | **Forced PWM (MODE = HIGH) costs 3 mA continuous** | [p. 7] | **HIGH** | If MODE is left HIGH (forced PWM) during sleep, the buck draws **3 mA continuous** instead of 60–150 nA. On a 300 mAh battery this drains the cell in ~4 days even with no other load. **Mitigation:** (a) v7.2 JP15 default **must** be GND (PFM/PWM auto). Verify silkscreen/schematic default. (b) Preferable for next revision: route MODE to an ESP-S3 GPIO so FW can control it explicitly, with default-LOW power-on state. (c) Add a power-budget sanity check at bench bring-up — measure I_BAT in deep-sleep and confirm < 10 µA total. |
| WARN-04 | **Battery near end-of-life drives 100 % duty mode — V_OUT follows V_IN minus drop** | [p. 15, §8.4.3] | MED | As Li-ion droops toward V_OUT setpoint (3.2 V), the buck enters 100 % mode (high-side FET always on). V_OUT = V_IN − I_OUT × (R_DSon + R_L). At 200 mA load and V_IN = 3.3 V, V_OUT drops to ≈ 3.16 V; at V_IN = 3.2 V, V_OUT = ≈ 3.06 V. The "3.2 V rail" is no longer 3.2 V — it tracks the battery. **Mitigation:** (a) loads on the 3.2 V rail (ESP-S3, sensors) must operate at V_DD as low as **3.0 V** to maintain function. Check sensor minimum V_DD specs (most are fine to 1.8 V). (b) Charger BQ25619 must enforce battery cutoff well before V_BAT < 3.2 V to keep the buck regulating properly. (c) FW should report battery state as "critical" when V_OUT < 3.15 V (read via ADC if Open Q3 implemented). |
| WARN-05 | **EN-pulldown re-arms after EN goes LOW — startup behavior depends on prior state** | [p. 12, §8.3.1] | LOW | The internal 450 kΩ pulldown on EN is disabled once a HIGH is detected, but re-enables once a LOW is seen. If a glitch toggles EN low, the next rising edge again undergoes the t_STARTUP_DELAY + soft-start cycle (200 µs + 120–700 µs). **Mitigation:** v7.2's hardwire of EN to SYS is robust against single-event glitches. If GPIO-driving EN in a future rev, debounce in FW (≥ 100 µs) before re-asserting. |
| WARN-06 | **R_SET re-read on every UVLO recovery — VSET trace must stay clean** | [p. 13, §8.3.5] | LOW | If V_IN brown-outs below UVLO and recovers, the R2D converter re-reads R_SET. If the VSET trace has high parasitic capacitance (> 100 pF) or picks up noise during this window, V_OUT could land on a wrong code. **Mitigation:** keep VSET trace short, away from SW node; do **not** route VSET under switching components. Use a guard ground if necessary. |
| WARN-07 | **MODE pin must not float** | [p. 5; p. 12 §8.3.3] | LOW | Although MODE has no internal pulldown (unlike EN and STOP), it must be terminated. A floating MODE pin's behavior is undefined and can chatter between PFM/PWM. **Mitigation:** v7.2 JP15 must always connect MODE to a defined level (GND or V_IN). Confirm JP15 footprint has no "open-pin" valid state. |
| WARN-08 | **Output discharge does NOT activate on thermal shutdown** | [p. 14, §8.3.7, §8.3.8] | LOW | When the chip enters thermal shutdown (160 °C junction), the FETs turn off but the output discharge MOSFET stays inactive. V_OUT will droop slowly from C_OUT into the load. **Mitigation:** none firmware-side. Document for bench teams — a thermal-shutdown event won't crash the rail instantly; loads will see a slow brown-out. |
| WARN-09 | **VSET parasitic must stay ≤ 100 pF** | [p. 6, §7.3] | LOW | Datasheet caps C_VSET at 100 pF. Beyond this, the R2D conversion at startup may misread R_SET and select the wrong V_OUT code. **Mitigation:** keep VSET trace short (< 5 mm), avoid running it parallel to SW or VIN traces. | WARN-10 | **Soft-start under heavy load may extend up to 1.2 ms** | [p. 8, t_SS_ILIMF max] | LOW | If the load on the 3.2 V rail is already drawing current at t = 0 (e.g., a misbehaving sensor is still powered), the reduced-current-limit soft-start phase can extend up to **t_SS_ILIMF = 1200 µs max**. V_OUT ramp slope decreases. **Mitigation:** if FW cycles EN to recover from a fault, expect up to 1.2 ms for V_OUT to be in regulation; budget BOR / reset timing for downstream chips accordingly. |

## Open questions

- **Q1 — End-of-battery rail behavior.** As Li-ion droops below ≈ 3.34 V (under 200 mA load), the buck enters 100 % mode and the "3.2 V rail" actually tracks the battery. **What V_BAT threshold should the BQ25619 use to declare "battery dead"** so the 3.2 V rail doesn't drop below 3.0 V (or whatever the ESP-S3 and sensors tolerate)? **Action:** cross-check with [20.13 BQ25619 extract] once parsed and align v_bat_cutoff. [[project_kompic_mk1]]
- **Q2 — MODE pin: jumper-fixed or FW-controllable?** v7.2 currently uses JP15 (jumper) to set MODE. **Recommendation: route MODE to an ESP-S3 GPIO** in next board rev so FW can dynamically switch to forced-PWM during ADC/RF noise-sensitive windows, then back to PFM. Adds one GPIO, costs nothing in BOM, saves significant battery vs. always-PWM. **Action:** if v7.1 PCB is not yet committed, request the change. Otherwise log for v7.3.
- **Q3 — V_OUT health monitoring.** Currently no ADC sense on the 3.2 V rail. FW has no way to verify the rail is at 3.2 V, distinguish from 100 %-mode droop, or detect thermal shutdown. **Action:** add a 100 kΩ / 100 kΩ divider from V_OUT (3.2 V → 1.6 V into ESP-S3 ADC) in next rev. Costs 2 resistors. Even-better: route V_OUT directly to ESP-S3 ADC (3.2 V is just below the ADC max with 11-dB attenuation enabled).
- **Q4 — EN-pin control.** v7.2 hardwires EN to SYS so the buck is always on. **Should FW have a way to power-cycle the 3.2 V rail** (e.g., to recover a wedged sensor)? If yes, route EN to a GPIO (with a 100 kΩ pull-up to SYS for fail-safe). **Action:** decide for next board rev; not critical for v7.1 bring-up.
- **Q5 — R_SET BOM line — 100 kΩ vs 102 kΩ.** v7.2 says 100 kΩ; datasheet says 102 kΩ NOM. Both fall inside the 3.2 V code band (97.92–106.08 kΩ) if 1 % tolerance is honored. **Action:** Ivan — confirm BOM line, tolerance, temp coefficient. Bench-measure V_OUT at first silicon: must be 3.152–3.248 V at room temp. If outside, swap R_SET to 102 kΩ E96 part.
- **Q6 — STOP pin connection.** v7.2 doesn't clearly state how STOP is connected on the DLC package. **Must be tied to GND for normal operation** (it cannot float reliably even with the internal 450 kΩ pulldown — too noise-prone for a 70 µA mode trigger). **Action:** confirm STOP-pin connection on v7.2 schematic. If routable to a GPIO at low cost, do so — enables FW-controlled noise gating for ADC bursts.
- **Q7 — Inductor part number.** v7.2 BOM should specify the muRata DFE201612E-2R2M (2.0 × 1.6 × 1.2 mm, 116 mΩ DCR, ≥ 1.5 A I_SAT) or the smaller DFE201210S (2.0 × 1.2 × 1.0 mm). **Action:** confirm BOM line and footprint.
